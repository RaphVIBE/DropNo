Steel, priced like gold.
Fig. 11 · lunette octogonale · macro tapisserie

Caption IG

In 1971, Audemars Piguet asked Gérald Genta for something no haute horlogerie maison had dared: a luxury sports watch in steel. He delivered the drawing the next morning. An octagonal bezel held by eight visible screws, inspired by a diver's helmet. An integrated bracelet. A dial textured like woven cloth. The Royal Oak launched in 1972 at 3,300 Swiss francs, more than most gold dress watches of the time. The industry called it commercial suicide. Steel, priced like gold, is what saved haute horlogerie. Half a century later, the steel sports watch it invented is the one you cannot get at retail. The cure became the symptom.

Icon 2 Mer 01/07 · 12h30 · Atelier · Speedmaster Carrousel · respire
Atelier
NASA tortured six chronographs. One came home.
Fig. 12 · compteurs · fond cuir chaud

Caption IG

In 1965, NASA put out a call to around ten brands. Only a handful sent a chronograph. Then the torture began. 70°C for two days, spiked to 93°C, dropped to minus 18°C. Near vacuum. 40G shocks, six directions. Vibration sweeps that destroyed dials and warped crystals. One watch passed: the Omega Speedmaster. It flew on every crewed Apollo mission, and in 1970 it timed the 14-second engine burn that brought Apollo 13 home, when the onboard clocks were dead. It is still hand-wound. It is still in the catalogue. One of the few grails you can still walk in and buy.

LI-3 Jeu 02/07 · 09h00 · LinkedIn · Sealed bids LinkedIn FR + EN

Français

Les ventes les plus sophistiquées du marché de l'art ne se font pas dans une salle. Elles se font sous enveloppe. L'enchère ouverte optimise le spectacle : la montée des paddles, le marteau, l'adrénaline. L'offre scellée optimise autre chose : la vérité. Quand personne ne voit l'offre de personne, chacun écrit ce que la pièce vaut pour lui, pas un cran au-dessus de son rival. Les théoriciens des enchères le savent depuis longtemps ; l'immobilier de prestige, les fréquences télécom et les ventes privées de Christie's le pratiquent. Le format le plus silencieux est souvent le plus juste.

English

The most sophisticated sales in the art market do not happen in a room. They happen in envelopes. Open auctions optimise for drama: the paddles, the hammer, the adrenaline. Sealed bids optimise for something else: truth. When nobody sees anybody's offer, each buyer writes what the piece is worth to them, not one increment above a rival. Auction theorists have known this for decades; prime real estate, telecom spectrum and Christie's private sales practise it. The quietest format is often the fairest.

The most consequential auctions on earth happen in silence. Every week, the US Treasury sells billions in bonds by sealed bid. No room, no paddles. I Mer 08/07 · 12h30 · Atelier · Tourbillon Reel · le plus filmique
Atelier
It was never about accuracy.
Fig. 10 · cage de tourbillon

Caption IG

A tourbillon does not make a watch more accurate. It was patented in 1801 to counter gravity's effect on a pocket watch sitting vertically in a waistcoat. On your wrist, the advantage is mostly gone. So why do collectors pay six figures for one? Because watching a mechanism rotate on itself, sixty seconds per revolution, built from ninety-odd parts lighter than a feather, was never about accuracy.

LI-4 Jeu 09/07 · 09h00 · LinkedIn · Direct LinkedIn FR + EN

Français

Ce qu'une maison indépendante gagne à vendre en direct ne se mesure pas d'abord en marge. La couche retail avait un sens quand la découverte était physique : il fallait des vitrines. Aujourd'hui la découverte est numérique, mais la vente des pièces les plus désirables passe encore par des intermédiaires qui captent la donnée client, décident des allocations et revendent la rareté qu'ils n'ont pas créée. En direct, la maison sait enfin qui achète, choisit à qui va la pièce rare, et garde la relation. La marge est un bonus. Le contrôle est le produit.

English

What an independent maison gains by selling direct is not measured in margin first. The retail layer made sense when discovery was physical: you needed windows. Discovery is digital now, yet the most desirable pieces still flow through intermediaries who capture the customer data, decide the allocations, and resell a scarcity they did not create. Direct, the maison finally knows who buys, chooses who receives the rare piece, and keeps the relationship. Margin is a bonus. Control is the product.
Icon 3 Ven 10/07 · 18h30 · Provenance · Paul Newman Daytona Carrousel · accès fort
Provenance
Drive carefully. Me.
Fig. 13 · Daytona panda · gravure caseback

Caption IG

Joanne Woodward gave her husband a Rolex Daytona engraved with three words: "Drive carefully. Me." Paul Newman wore it every day for fifteen years, then handed it to his daughter's boyfriend, who needed a watch. In 2017, that Daytona sold at Phillips for 17.8 million dollars, at the time the most expensive wristwatch ever auctioned. The watch was standard production. Anyone could have walked in and bought one. Everything that made it priceless happened after they left the boutique.
