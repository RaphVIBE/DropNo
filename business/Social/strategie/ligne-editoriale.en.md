## Editorial positioning

A watchmaking notebook, not a brand account. We write like a knowledgeable collector sharing what he knows, never like a company preparing a launch. Authority comes from the precision of the details (dates, temperatures, figures), not from volume.

**Languages**: public feed (IG, X, TikTok) in pure EN with FR touches (maison, calibre, №, grand feu); #watchfam is English-speaking and we do not fragment a nascent audience. LinkedIn bilingual FR then EN in the same post: the commercial target (Romandy and French maisons) reads in its own language. FR/EN by user preference on the site and newsletter at reveal.

## Visual system

- **Typographic cards** (signature): thesis posts. Light during stealth. Template in `social-kit.html`. Each card carries a "technical plate" watermark (Fig. 01, 02… numbered continuously) in the style of an old watchmaking treatise, with FR annotations and a motif tied to the post's subject.
- **Macro photos** (reach): 1-2 per week alternating within the rubrics, never the same day as a card. Dials, calibres, serial engravings. Desaturated, warm light, never a lifestyle wristshot. Sources: Unsplash/Pexels (verify license) then partner-maison photos. Caption identical to the card system.
- **Tease switch**: everything goes dark (ink + champagne) on 31/08. The visual break IS the signal.

## Writing rules

- Short sentences. We state. Never an exclamation mark, never an emoji in the feed, no em dashes.
- Every post ends on a closing line, not a CTA. No "follow for more", no "what do you think?".
- Stealth bans: the words "platform", "marketplace", "launch", "startup", any mention of the sealed-bid/uniform-price mechanism before tier 3, any link to the site before reveal.
- Standard reply in comments if asked who we are: "Field notes on watchmaking. The rest, in time."
- LinkedIn may go one notch further ("some of us are working on exactly that"), never more.
