Dear Nicholas,

First, with genuine admiration: reviving Fears and carrying it back to Bristol is one of the more quietly impressive stories in independent watchmaking. The Brunswick, the in-house cushion case, the restraint of the whole thing. And your Boutique Editions, tiny runs of around ten pieces, sell with a directness most brands would envy.

Those ten-piece editions are, almost to the letter, the format I built Drop No. around. The piece missing is a price mechanism, and one detail caught my attention: those editions are largely out of reach for collectors on the continent.

I recently launched Drop No., a sealed-bid drop platform for brand-direct watchmaking. The mechanism is one line: a few examples of a piece, five days of hidden bids, reveal on Thursday at 6pm. The highest bids win, and all winners pay the same price, that of the last winning bid. No public auction, no race, no immediate flipping.

With it comes data no study can give you: what your collectors were genuinely willing to pay for a given edition, the full distribution, the profile of verified buyers. For a continental Brunswick edition, that is a clean way to test real EU demand before committing to it.

You keep the floor price, the calendar, the number of pieces. Below the floor, the drop is cancelled: no fees, nothing sold cheap. The watch only leaves your workshop to reach a verified buyer, shipped insured, we never hold stock. Our commission, twelve percent, applies only to actual sales.

One honest note: a UK-to-EU drop means clearing customs and VAT cleanly on the insured shipment, something I would want to map with you before anything is committed.

I would be glad to prepare a simulation in your name rather than just describe it. Fifteen minutes by video this week or next?

Kind regards,
